About Me
I'm a researcher at ARO, Inc in Seattle, WA.

I earned my PhD in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Washington. My advisors were Gaetano Borriello and James Fogarty. My dissertation research focused on how people use food diaries on mobile phones. More generally, I'm interested in how technology can help people live richer, more meaningful lives. While at UW, I was honored to be able to intern at Intel Research Seattle (with Beverly Harrison) and Microsoft Research (with Amy Karlson and A.J. Brush).

Before returning to graduate school, I worked at Pacific Northwest National Laboratory for a number of years. My work there included collaborations around rich interaction environments, knowledge systems, and usability of visualizations.

I also earned an MA in Learning, Design and Technology from Stanford University and a BS in Computer Science (VI-3) from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
